Pad Thai

A Pad Thai Worth Making

The wonderful national noodle dish of Thailand, it is now served in many trendy restaurants. It can be adjusted to your taste, add ingredients that you like and make it as spicy or tart as you want to.

It is also great as a basis for a stir fry of leftovers. This is a recipe for those who like it HOT, if you can't handle the heat, go easy on the chili peppers.

Ingredients

  • 1 package dried rice noodles
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1/4 vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp chopped garlic
  • 200g chicken breast
  • 3 cups bean sprouts
  • 6 medium green onions
  • 2 tbsp chopped peanuts
  • 1 green Thai chili pepper

Steps

  • Step 1

    • Fill a large bowl with hot tap water and place the noodles in it to soak for 20 minutes.

  • Step 2

    • In a small bowl, stir together the fish sauce, lime juice, sugar, oyster sauce, 2 teaspoons of the chile sauce and chicken stock.

    Set aside.

  • Step 3

    • Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat and add vegetable oil.

    When the oil is hot, stir in garlic and cook for about 10 seconds. Add the chicken; cook, stirring constantly until cooked through, 5 to 7 minutes.

  • Step 4

    • Add the noodles to the wok and pour in the sauce.

    Cook, stirring constantly, until the noodles are tender.

    Add a bit more water if needed to finish cooking the noodles.

    Stir in 3 cups of bean sprouts and green onions. Remove from the heat and garnish with chopped peanuts.

    Taste for seasoning, adjusting the spice or lime juice if needed.

  • Step 5

    • Serve garnished with lime wedges on the side.

    Enjoy!
